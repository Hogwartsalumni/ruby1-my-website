The Beatles


Ringo Starr (Richard Starkey)

Richard Starkey was born on July 7, 1940 in Liverpool, England.
As a child he suffered from many illnesses and lived in the hospital
most of his childhood. He missed so many hours of school, it forced him
to drop out at the age of 16. Ringo learned to drum at his time in the
hospital and started a band. He eventually joined one of Liverpool's
hottest bands, Rory Strom and The Hurricanes, as the drummer. In
1962, he replaced The Beatles drummer, Pete Best. Ringo contributed
lots to The Beatles. At age 77, Ringo continues ot spread peace and
love and play music aroudn the world.
